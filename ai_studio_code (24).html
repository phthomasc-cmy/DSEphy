üå°Ô∏è The Temperature Difference ($\Delta T$)

Calculated as $(T_{hot} - T_{cold})$. The bigger the difference, the faster heat runs! It's the "pushing force" for heat.

üß± Thermal Conductivity ($\kappa$)

This tells us how easily a material transmits heat. High $\kappa$ (like Copper) = Fast heat. Low $\kappa$ (like Styrofoam) = Slow heat.

üìê Area ($A$)

A larger surface area gives the heat more room to escape. It is directly proportional to power ($P$).

üìè Thickness ($d$)

This is on the bottom of the fraction (denominator). The thicker the wall, the slower the rate of conduction.
